About Us

Female hygiene is a severely overlooked health issue in America and I want to bring a steady end to it. Living in the city has taught me how to be frugal, yet simply maintaining one’s hygiene, especially as a female, has been quite expensive and inconvenient. I live in a neighborhood where I encounter hundreds of female homeless mothers, teenagers, young women and women in poverty who struggle with affording basic female hygiene products such as sanitary napkins, tampons, adult incontinence products, and other feminine needs products. The average cost of a feminine product ranges from $5 to $9 depending on the size and type of the package. A year’s supply of tampons and pads cost upwards of 100$ and is not covered by food stamps. For homeless women, the lack of access to menstrual hygiene care is often compounded due to the lack of sanitary spaces like toilets and showers.
